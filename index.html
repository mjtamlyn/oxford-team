The Oxford Team Tournament

An exciting new shoot coming January 2013

The Oxford Team Tournament is a new archery competition designed for teams. Three archers shoot a FITA 18m qualification round, followed by team knock out matches, using Beiter Hit-Miss targets.

Let me know when entries open

The competition is open to teams of three archers, either all recurve or all compound. The ranking round is a normal FITA 18, shot on 3 spot faces to record status timings. The matches which follow consist of 24 arrows per team shot over 4 ends of 2 minutes each. Highest number of hits wins. The matches are shot on Beiter Hit Miss targets, 50mm for recurves and 30mm for compounds. These targets are a great spectacle, and hugely fun to shoot at. For an example of what they're like, check out the final of the British Universities Team Championships 2012 on YouTube.

Teams must represent something, for example a club, university, alumni group, shop team or family. National teams and scratch teams of friends are not permitted. Acceptance of entry is at the discretion of the tournament committee.
